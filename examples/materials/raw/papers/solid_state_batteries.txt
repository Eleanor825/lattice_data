# Solid-State Batteries for High-Safety Energy Storage

Solid-state batteries replace flammable liquid electrolytes with solid ionic conductors. This shift can improve safety, widen electrochemical stability windows, and enable the use of lithium metal anodes in practical devices.

Recent studies show that sulfide and oxide electrolytes exhibit different tradeoffs in conductivity, interface stability, and manufacturability. High-quality datasets for materials models should preserve these distinctions together with synthesis conditions and measurement evidence.
